Summary: In this chapter, Dorothea Brooke (now Mrs. Casaubon) and her husband, Mr. Casaubon, are on their honeymoon in Rome. Will Ladislaw, who is related to Mr. Casaubon, sees Dorothea at an art museum and is captivated by her beauty. He discusses his thoughts with his German friend, Adolf Naumann, who suggests that he paint a portrait of Dorothea. However, Ladislaw is hesitant and dismisses the idea. The chapter ends with Ladislaw feeling strangely affected by Dorothea.
