Summary: Will Ladislaw debates whether his decision to settle in Middlemarch was a mistake. He decides to go to Lowick Church to see Dorothea and defies Mr. Casaubon's prohibition. However, when he sees Dorothea, he feels uncomfortable and realizes he made a blunder. Will feels miserable throughout the service and is unable to look at Dorothea. After the service, Dorothea and Mr. Casaubon leave without acknowledging Will.

It happened to be on a Saturday evening that Will Ladislaw had that little discussion with Lydgate. Its effect when he went to his own rooms was to make him sit up half the night, thinking over again, under a new irritation, all that he had before thought of his having settled in Middlemarch and harnessed himself with Mr. Brooke.
